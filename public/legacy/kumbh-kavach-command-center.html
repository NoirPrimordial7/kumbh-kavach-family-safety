📋 PRESENTATION NOTES

🔱 What is Kumbh Kavach?

Kumbh Kavach is a smart crowd management system designed for large religious gatherings like the Kumbh Mela. It uses two physical devices — Smart Mauli wristbands worn by pilgrims, and Smart Bamboo LED panels at entry gates — to monitor crowd density, detect dangers, and guide people away from dangerous zones in real time, without requiring phones or internet.

⚠️ What Problem Does It Solve?

At Kumbh 2003, 39 people died in a stampede. At Kumbh 2013, 36 people died. These weren't accidents — they were predictable. Crowds built up past the danger threshold, but no system could detect and respond fast enough. Traditional methods rely on human observers and police radios — too slow, too late.

💡 Say: "The tragedy at Kumbh has happened multiple times. We're here to make sure it never happens again."

🔄 How Does Rerouting Work?

When a ghat zone hits 70% capacity (our early warning threshold), the system automatically checks which nearby zones have space. It then flashes LED arrows on the Smart Bamboo gates — pointing pilgrims toward the safer route. Simultaneously, the wristband LED display updates from "R-A" (Route A) to "R-B" (Route B). Pilgrims follow the band — no phone, no announcement, no panic.

💡 Demo: Run "Shahi Snan Surge" and point to the gate arrows flashing on the map.

⛺ What Do The Camps Do?

The system includes four support nodes visible on the map. The Medical Camp activates when a pilgrim's band detects a cardiac event — it coordinates ambulance routing and clears a corridor. The Volunteer Camp activates when a child goes missing — all nearby volunteers are notified instantly. The Police Camp activates for VIP movement or emergency control. The Overflow Camp receives redirected pilgrims when main ghats are too full.

📈 What Happens in a Surge? (Shahi Snan — Full Surge)

This is the hero scenario — run it for your main demo. It has 12 stages across ~60 seconds of simulation time. Stage 1: Panchavati Entry opens, crowd flows in. Stage 2: Ahilyadevi fills steadily. Stage 3: Ahilyadevi hits warning (yellow glow). Stage 4: Ahilyadevi goes DANGER — G4 CLOSES. Stage 5: Back-pressure builds at Panchavati Entry — people queuing. Stage 6: Rerouting activates — crowd sent to Makhmalabad and Ramkund. Stage 7: Ramkund rises from redirected crowd. Stage 8: Dual crisis — both ghats red — Tapovan and Overflow Camp receive. Stage 9: Lost child sub-event — Volunteer Camp activates. Stage 10: Cardiac event — Medical Camp + Hospital activate. Stage 11: Recovery — G4 reopens, zones stabilize. Stage 12: All clear — zero casualties.

💡 Say: "Notice how the system doesn't just react — it also stops new people from entering a zone that's already full. That back-pressure logic is what prevents the queue from becoming a crush."

🚧 What Happens When a Gate Fails?

Run "Gate Failure" to see: Gate G3 loses power (hardware failure). The system detects this within 3 seconds (it monitors heartbeats from each gate panel). It immediately re-plans routes — pilgrims going Ahilyadevi → Makhmalabad are directed via Gate G5 (alternate path). Maintenance is auto-alerted. When G3 comes back online, it's tested automatically before being reactivated.

🌐 Why Is This Useful for Kumbh?

Kumbh Mela hosts 50 million pilgrims over 45 days. Many are elderly, without phones, and come from rural areas with no digital literacy. Our system works with zero smartphone dependency, works offline (bands use BLE, gates use local mesh), scales to thousands simultaneously, and is designed to deploy in 14 days before any major event.
